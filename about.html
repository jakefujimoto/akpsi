The Brooklyn Four

The story of Alpha Kappa Psi begins at New York University, Washington Square, New York. In the fall of 1902, four men, George L. Bergen, Nathan Lane Jr, Frederic R. Leach, and Howard M. Jefferson, occupied the same seats in five night classes a week. The four met each night after classes to take advantage of the quietness of lower Broadway to walk to City Hall and then across the Brooklyn Bridge to their homes. These eager, serious-minded students later became the founders and first elected members of Alpha Kappa Psi Fraternity. They acquired a new name and were widely heralded as the Brooklyn Four.
